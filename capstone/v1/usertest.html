Usability Test Results: Light is Pollution

Key Observations

1. The scroll interaction was intuitive, and the tester immediately tried scrolling without any prompt. The visual transitions between stages made the progress feel satisfying.

2. The changing text was noticed, but the tester mentioned it could be emphasized more (e.g., fade-in or typewriter animation) to draw focus to the changing narrative.

3. The tester expected clickable elements, especially on the glowing buildings. They hovered over them expecting tooltips or interactivity.

Evaluation

Areas that need improvement: The emphasis on changing text can be stronger, and a small visual cue could help guide users to scroll. The background images could also fade more smoothly between transitions.

Was navigation clear? Yes. The scroll-based interaction was naturally understood and used by the tester without confusion.

Was the message effective? Mostly. The tester grasped the narrative arc but suggested the emotion behind the loss of stars could be pushed further with sound or more immersive visuals.

Modifications to be made:

  • Fade or animate title/subtitle text to emphasize changes
  • Add a scroll cue or animated arrow at the beginning
  • Consider hover effects or subtle interactivity on the city buildings
  • Smooth transitions between background images

Plan for Updates

Based on testing feedback, I will implement the following:

  • Use a fade-in or typewriter effect to introduce each new stage’s text
  • Add an animated arrow or glow effect at the bottom to prompt scrolling
  • Optional: Add subtle sound transitions or ambient background audio
  • Refine image transitions with opacity or crossfade effects
